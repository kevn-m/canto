# Chibi mascot sprite pipeline

All sprites in this folder are generated by `sprites.js` тАФ a shape-recipe system, not hand-placed pixels. To add subjects, append recipes to `sprites.js`; never draw pixel grids by hand or use image tools. Export locally with `node render.js` (writes `<name>.png` for every recipe and audits the palette).

## Spec (hard rules тАФ repeat-check every batch)
- 64├Ч64 PNG, transparent background, one centred subject filling ~70тАУ80%
- PICO-8 palette only (defined as `P` in sprites.js); audit after export
- 1px navy `#1D2B53` outline (automatic pass), never black
- Chibi: head тЙИ half sprite height, no neck, stubby limbs
- Exaggerate exactly 2 signature features; simplify everything else
- Mouth turns UP, always visible. Eyes large navy ovals + cream sparkle, set low/wide. Optional pink blush.
- No text, nothing scary, must pass silhouette test (render with `drawTo(ctx, name, true)`)
- Light from top-left; one shadow tone + max one highlight tone per surface; no dithering/gradients/AA

## The one exception: FX sprites (`fx-*`)

Battle effects тАФ `fx-slash`, `fx-impact` тАФ are the only sprites with no face and no
chibi armature. They are shapes, not creatures; eyes on a slash arc would be absurd.
Palette, outline and no-AA still apply. Everything else in the spec above does not.

## The other exception: the Rig and its gear layers

`rig-base`, `avatar-*` and the gear layers (`helm-*`, `chest-*`, `legs-*`, `weap-*`,
`off-*`) draw against the shared `RIG` zones (`sprites.js`), not the "one centred
subject filling ~70-80%" rule тАФ a gear layer is deliberately a small shape sitting in
one corner of an otherwise empty 64px canvas, because compositing is stacking
same-sized layers, not scaling one drawing. Gear layers also have no face; a helmet
or a sword doesn't need eyes. Palette, outline and no-AA still apply.

**The Rig looks like everything else.** It is the standard round chibi armature тАФ
`player-kid`'s big head on a small body тАФ not a style of its own.

A sharper, angular, "more heroic" Rig was built and REJECTED. It looked fine alone
and wrong in the game: the player stood next to 2,400 round chibi enemies and card
sprites and read as a different game. Don't try it again. If an avatar needs to look
cool, it comes from hair, palette and gear тАФ never from a different silhouette.

- **Use `ball()`, like every other sprite.** Round and soft is the house style.
- **Use the normal face kit** (`eyes`, `smileArc`, `blush`) via `rigEyes()`.
- **Call `rigBody()` and `rigFace()`.** An avatar varies its head, hair and palette and
  nothing else тАФ that is what makes gear fit every avatar for one drawing instead of
  one drawing per avatar (ADR 0025). A new avatar that draws its own body has broken
  the Rig, even if it looks fine on its own.
- **The head is FLAT peach тАФ pass no shadow colour.** `ball()`'s shade rim wraps under
  the mouth and reads as a beard; `player-kid` has said so since batch 1.
- **Nothing on an avatar's head may rise above `RIG.helm.y0`.** The helm's crown starts
  there and anything taller clips through it.
- **The helm stops at `RIG.helm.y1`; the eyes start at y23.** Read the zone, don't retype
  it тАФ a hand-typed brim already drifted a row past the line once. A helm that covers
  the face makes every avatar the same knight, which defeats having avatars.
- The Rig grows stubby legs that `player-kid` doesn't have, only because the leggings
  slot needs somewhere to sit.

## The kit (in sprites.js)
- `ball(g,cx,cy,rx,ry,base,shad,hi)` тАФ self-shading ellipse (shadow rim bottom-right, light top-left). The workhorse.
- `ellipse/disc/rect/rrect/tri` тАФ flat fills
- `stroke(g,pts,r0,r1,c)` тАФ tapered polyline of discs: trunks, tails, steam. For a limb that needs its own outline against same-colour body, stroke navy at r+1 first, then the colour (see elephant trunk).
- `clipTo(g,[colors],fn)` тАФ paint only over listed colours: stripes, patches
- `eyes/smileArc/openMouth/blush` тАФ face kit; `smileArc(g,cx,y,w,depth)` ends curve UP
- `outline(g)` тАФ auto navy dilation, runs last; erasing with `null` (bite notches, handle holes) gets outlined correctly
- `CX = 31.5` тАФ centre; use for all symmetric placement

## Two ways the kit will bite you

- **Give `eyes()` a whole-number `ry`.** `eye()` places its cream sparkle at `y - ry + 1`,
  and `Grid.set` indexes `y * 64 + x` without flooring. A half-integer `ry` therefore lands
  on a real but WRONG pixel тАФ a stray cream speck ~32px across the canvas, outside the
  sprite. (Other fractions just drop the sparkle silently.) `ry: 2` is safe, `ry: 2.5` is not.
- **Draw a head flat тАФ pass no shadow colour.** `ball()`'s shadow rim wraps the bottom of
  the head, and a dark rim under a mouth reads as a beard or a droopy moustache. `player-kid`
  has said so in a comment since batch 1; the Rig relearned it the hard way.

## Standard armature (deviate only for signature features)
head ball (CX, 25, 15, 13) ┬╖ body ball (CX, 48, ~10, 8) ┬╖ feet balls at x 25/38, y 56 ┬╖ eyes yтЙИ25 spread 6тАУ8 ┬╖ muzzle cyтЙИ31 ┬╖ smile yтЙИ32 ┬╖ blush at spread+5, yтЙИ30. Draw order: back features (ears/tail/fins) тЖТ body тЖТ limbs тЖТ head тЖТ face.

## Colour ramps (base тЖТ shadow, optional highlight)
skyтЖТlav ┬╖ lgyтЖТlav (hi crm) ┬╖ orgтЖТbrn (hi yel) ┬╖ yelтЖТorg (hi crm) ┬╖ brnтЖТplum ┬╖ limтЖТgrn ┬╖ redтЖТplum (hi pnk) ┬╖ crmтЖТlgy. Keep highlights ONLY on low-contrast pairs тАФ high-contrast highlights read as blotches (learned: removed from dolphin/bear/croc).

## Workflow for a new batch
1. Append recipes to `S` in sprites.js (name + draw fn, ~15 lines each).
2. `node render.js` тАФ regenerates every PNG in this folder and fails loud on any out-of-palette colour.
3. Iterate coordinates 1тАУ3 rounds. Common fixes: detached appendages (move within 1px of body), same-colour features vanishing (navy underlay stroke), highlight blotches (remove hi).
4. Silhouette-check new sprites, then get the batch approved before bundling into the app.
